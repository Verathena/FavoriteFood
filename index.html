Garri (Cassave Flakes)

About Garri

It is unclear where or when Garri originated, but it is safe to say that Garri has existed as far back as human memory serves. Garri is made from fermented cassava in a long and arduous process which this recipe will not go into, ostensibly because it is long and arduous (as previously stated, in case you missed it the first time), but really because the writers do not know.
Garri is one of Nigeria's favorite staples, second only in popularity to rice. It is commonly referred to as Garium Sulphate by students in an attempt to make it sound scientific, even though it contains little to no sodium at all. Garri is beloved by Saparians because it is cheap, easy to consume and saves time. Garri slaps when it is good, is delicious, refreshing, and is a life-saver.
Garri is best enjoyed when it is taken with really cold water, especially when the temperature outside is high. There are quite a few ways to take Garri, depending on things like budget, how high (or low) you are on the sapa food chain, the color of your bank account, and ultimately your preference. Garri can be taken by ajepakos and butties. The recipe below is for entry-level or junior Saparians. Senior and C-level Saparians should waka pass.

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Garri 1/2 Cup
Powdered Milk 5 Heaped Spoonfuls
Water 2 Cups
Sugar 2 Cubes
Ice Cubes 10 Cubes
Groundnut 1 Handful

Steps

  1. Pour the garri inside a clean cup
  2. Add water up to a cup, and stir vigorously but quickly
  3. Gently decant until garri is all that is left in the cup.
  4. Pour the 10 ice cubes in the cup
  5. Add the powdered milk, sugar and groundnut in no particular order
  6. Stir till all is evenly mixed
  7. Enjoy
